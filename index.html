Features of fig tree of Benjamin

In nature, Ficus Benjamin is found in India, Southern China, the Philippines, Malaysia and Northern Australia. The size of the bush depends on the growth conditions and the variety, and it can vary from 50 to 300 centimeters. On average, within 1 year the bush increases in height by 20 centimeters.

When grown at home, you will not see such a shrub blooming. However, in greenhouses it can form syconia — these are ball‑shaped flowers, they look similar to berries.

This shrub is a perennial, which is distinguished by its unpretentiousness in care and undemanding conditiouns of growing. It is cultivated as an ornamental foliage plante.
